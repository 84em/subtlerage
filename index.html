Subtlerage

Subtlerage is the musical moniker of Andrew Miller, a composer and ambient electronic artist active primarily in the 1990s and 2000s. His work explores introspective soundscapes and ambient minimalism, blending organic textures with moody electronics.

Latest Work: Reflections (2004)

The culmination of years of evolution, Reflections blends acoustic piano, string duets, and ambient electronics. It’s a mature, immersive album showcasing Subtlerage’s full emotional and stylistic range.

Early Works

Andrew Miller's earliest work as Subtlerage includes two obscure releases from the mid-to-late 1990s: First Impressions and Vicious Cycle. These out-of-print albums showcased a raw, lo-fi approach to ambient experimentation that laid the foundation for his signature sound.

Random Acts of Insanity (1999)

Subtlerage’s first widely available album, Random Acts of Insanity, was released in 1999 through Noishole Records. This 13-track album introduced a darker, more immersive ambient tone with dense textures and post-IDM experimentation.

Meditations (2002)

Considered Subtlerage’s signature work, Meditations delivers a serene, emotionally rich ambient experience.

Legacy

Though never commercial, Subtlerage built a small, loyal following with his atmospheric music. His catalog—spanning introspective beginnings to mature compositions—remains available on streaming platforms like Spotify and SoundCloud, where new listeners continue to discover his deeply personal sound.
